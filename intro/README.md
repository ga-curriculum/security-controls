## What is the CIA Triad?

The CIA Triad is the security professional's North Star—a model that guides all our security decisions.

- **Confidentiality**

  - Ensures information is accessible only to authorized parties
  - Prevents sensitive data exposure
  - *Example:* Encryption keeping your bank account details private

- **Integrity**

  - Guarantees data remains accurate and unaltered
  - Detects unauthorized modifications
  - *Example:* Hash verification confirming your downloaded software hasn't been tampered with

- **Availability**

  - Ensures systems and data are accessible when needed
  - Prevents service disruptions
  - *Example:* Redundant servers keeping your favorite streaming service online 24/7

Every security control you implement should map back to at least one aspect of the CIA Triad. If it doesn't, ask yourself if it's truly providing value.

## Introduction to Security Controls

Security controls are safeguards or countermeasures designed to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of systems and data. Think of them as your security toolbox where each control has a specific purpose in your overall defense strategy.

### Security Controls Framework

Security controls can be categorized in two primary ways:

- **By Category** (what organizational area they fall under):

  - Managerial (Administrative)
  - Operational
  - Technical
  - Physical

- **By Function** (how they act in your security ecosystem):

  - Preventive
  - Detective
  - Corrective
  - Compensating
